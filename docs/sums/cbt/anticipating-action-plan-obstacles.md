## Anticipating and Preventing Action Plan Obstacles

The most important question: **"How likely are you to do this, 0–100%?"**

If the client says less than 90% confident, don't assume they'll do it. Investigate obstacles now.

**Identifying the gap:** When Maria says she's 75% sure, ask:
- "What's the 25% part of you that thinks you won't do it?"
- "Why 75% and not 50%?"
- "What could we do to get you from 75% to 95%?"
- "What are the advantages and disadvantages of doing this Action Plan?"

**Types of obstacles to explore:**

*Practical problems:* Lack of time, energy, transportation, unclear instructions. Solution: problem-solve together, simplify the task, or change timing.

*Interfering automatic thoughts:* "I'll mess it up," "It won't help anyway," "I'm too tired." Solution: identify and respond to the thought, or record a coping statement on the Action Plan.

*Skill deficits:* Client doesn't know how to do the task. Solution: teach or practice the skill in session.

**Covert rehearsal technique:** Ask the client to vividly imagine doing the task from start to finish. Have them narrate what they see, feel, and think at each step. When an obstacle or negative thought arises in the imagery, help them generate a response. This mental rehearsal exposes hidden barriers and preps coping strategies.

**Make it a no-lose proposition (initially):** In early sessions, tell clients: "If you complete this Action Plan, that's great. But if you have trouble, that's okay—we'll just figure out what thoughts got in the way and talk about them next session." This reduces shame and keeps the focus on learning.
