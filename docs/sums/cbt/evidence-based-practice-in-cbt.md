## Applying Evidence-Based Practice (EBP)

Evidence-Based Practice (EBP) is a core principle that guides ethical and effective therapy. It is defined as the integration of the best available research evidence with the specific context of the client to inform every stage of clinical decision-making and service delivery. This approach moves beyond relying on a single modality and instead focuses on a three-part framework:

1.  **Best Available Research:** Using therapeutic interventions and strategies that have been shown through empirical research to be effective.
2.  **Client Characteristics & Culture:** Tailoring the intervention to the individual client's specific characteristics, values, and cultural background. What works in a research trial may need adaptation to be effective for a particular person.
3.  **Client Preferences:** Respecting and incorporating the client's own treatment preferences and goals into the plan.

EBP is consistent with the highest ethical codes and professional standards. Its primary goal is to ensure that the services provided are cost-effective, maximize benefit, and minimize risk to the client. This requires a commitment to continuous professional development, as therapists must stay informed about current research on interventions and treatment strategies to provide the best possible care.
