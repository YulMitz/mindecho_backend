## The Skateboard Model for Youth CBT

The "Skate into Skills" model, developed by Tania Cargo and Nikki Coleman, uses the familiar object of a skateboard as a metaphor to conceptualize and apply CBT strategies with young people in New Zealand. This approach helps to externalize the client's experience, making it easier to understand and work with collaboratively.

The model breaks down a challenging experience into manageable parts, represented by the skateboard:

*   **The Deck:** Represents the **Situation** or activating event the young person is reacting to.
*   **The Four Wheels:** Each wheel represents a component of the reaction:
    1.  **Thoughts** (cognitions)
    2.  **Feelings** (emotions)
    3.  **Physical Reactions** (body sensations)
    4.  **Behaviors** (actions)
*   **The Skate Park:** Represents the broader **Environment** and systemic factors (e.g., family, school, community) that influence the situation and the client's reactions.

The therapeutic process follows a clear, step-by-step procedure:
1.  **Select a Situation:** The therapist and client choose one challenging situation to focus on, which is written on the skateboard deck.
2.  **Identify the Components:** They identify the thoughts, feelings, physical reactions, and behaviors connected to that situation and write them on the four wheels.
3.  **Consider Systemic Factors:** They look for outside factors in the "skate park" that may need to be addressed.
4.  **Develop Strategies:** The client develops targeted CBT strategies for each area they feel ready to change (e.g., challenging a thought, using a relaxation technique for a physical reaction, trying a new behavior).
5.  **Create a New Skateboard:** The client creates a new skateboard diagram, adding these new tools and strategies.
6.  **Test the Ride:** The client predicts what the "ride" will be like with the new tools and then tests it out in the real world, reporting back on the experience in the next session.

This metaphor provides a concrete, engaging, and empowering framework for applying CBT with young people.
