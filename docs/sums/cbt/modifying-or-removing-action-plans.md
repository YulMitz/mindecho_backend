## Changing the Action Plan

If you judge an Action Plan inappropriate, or if clients remain unsure they'll do it despite your efforts, modify it.

**When to modify:**
- The item is too difficult for the client's current state (cognitive impairment, low motivation, fatigue)
- The instructions were unclear or ill-defined
- The client's confidence drops below 70–80%
- Real-world obstacles make it impossible

**How to modify:**
- Substitute an easier Action Plan the client is likely to complete
- Reduce frequency, duration, or difficulty
- Make the item optional
- Break it into smaller steps

**The reasoning:** It's better to have clients complete a smaller, achievable task than to establish a pattern of non-compliance. Success builds momentum; repeated failure erodes hope and alliance.

**How to frame it:** "I'm not sure you're ready for this, or I'm not sure this Action Plan is appropriate. What do you think? Do you want to go ahead and try, or should we wait and try something different?"

You can also take responsibility: "Now that we've talked about it, I can see that I didn't explain this well enough, or it was really too hard. I'm sorry. What went through your mind when you couldn't do it?" This models that therapists make mistakes and can adjust—normalizing the process and protecting the alliance.
