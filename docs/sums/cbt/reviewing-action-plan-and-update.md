## Update and Action Plan Review

**Purpose**: The update bridges the previous session and the current one. It acknowledges progress, identifies what helped, and prepares the client to continue or modify their between-session work.

### Starting with Positive Experiences

Rather than opening with "What went wrong this week?", start with recovery-oriented questions:
- "What happened this week that was good?"
- "What did you do this week that was good?"
- "When were you at your best this week?"
- "What was the best part of the week?"

This serves several functions:
- Counters the depression-driven focus on negatives
- Helps clients see the week more accurately (they didn't suffer every moment)
- Creates positive emotion in the session, putting them in a better mindset
- Gives you concrete examples of meaningful activities to reinforce and potentially expand

### Extracting Learning from Positive Experiences

When a client reports something positive, don't just move on. Use it to teach:
- **Praise their effort**: "I'm glad you were able to do that."
- **Ask what was good about it**: "What was good about taking your grandchildren to the concert?"
- **Elicit positive conclusions**: "What did you learn from that experience?"
- **Link to their capabilities**: "What does it say about you that even though you're so depressed and tired, you got yourself to go?"

This helps clients develop adaptive beliefs about themselves—that they can push through difficulty, that they're capable of meaningful action, that their depression doesn't define them.

### Reviewing the Action Plan

Once positive experiences are discussed, ask about the specific between-session tasks from last week:
- "Were you able to read your therapy notes twice a day?"
- "Did you get out every day?"
- "Did you give yourself credit when you read the notes?"

For each item:
1. Acknowledge completion with positive reinforcement
2. Ask about the meaning of their actions ("Did your daughter appreciate that?")
3. Discuss whether to continue the item next week
4. Identify opportunities to expand ("Should we add babysitting to your plan again?")

### Assessing Willingness and Sustainability

Before moving to new agenda items, ensure the client isn't overwhelmed. If they completed 90% of one task, that's excellent. Recognize that consistency matters more than perfection.

### Staying Alert for New Agenda Items

During the update, new issues often surface. Note them and ask: "Should we put that on the agenda for today?" This prevents important issues from being lost while respecting the client's priorities.
