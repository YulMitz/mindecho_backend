## Action Plan Reminder Systems

Once you've agreed on an Action Plan, establish a concrete reminder system. Without one, clients forget—especially when depressed or anxious.

**Pair tasks with daily habits:** Link the Action Plan to something already automatic. For example, "How about jotting down what you deserve credit for at mealtimes and right before bed?" Habit stacking increases adherence.

**Physical reminders:**
- Post sticky notes on the refrigerator, bathroom mirror, computer, or car dashboard
- Put notes in the appointment book or on the calendar

**Phone and technology cues:**
- Set an alarm on their cell phone (suggest doing this together in session)
- Use a timer or device reminder
- Write the task in their phone's notes or calendar app

**Wearable cues:** For tasks to remember throughout the day (like monitoring thoughts or giving self-credit), suggest wearing a rubber band on the wrist, switching a watch to the other wrist, or wearing an unfamiliar bracelet. Each time they notice their wrist, they think of the task.

**Accountability to others:** Ask if another person (friend, family member) could remind them.

**Practical inquiry:** Ask how they remember other regular activities (taking medication, vitamins). Use those same systems for therapy homework.

The goal is to move the task from conscious willpower to environmental structure. What works varies by person—collaborate to find their system.
