## Checking Likelihood of Completion

Before ending your discussion of any Action Plan, ask: **"How likely are you to do this, 0–100%?"**

This simple quantitative question provides critical information. If the client says:
- **90–100%:** Confidence is high. Proceed.
- **70–89%:** Moderate hesitation. Investigate obstacles (see *Anticipating Action Plan Obstacles*).
- **Below 70%:** Low confidence. Either modify the plan or intensify your problem-solving.

**Why this matters:** Research on therapy outcomes shows that client commitment to between-session tasks strongly predicts progress. A number below 90% is a red flag. It tells you there's a cognition, practical barrier, skill gap, or alliance issue you haven't addressed yet.

**Quick diagnostic:** The gap between 100% and their number reveals the obstacle. If they say 75%, that 25% gap is usually either a practical problem ("I don't have time") or a thought ("I'll probably mess it up anyway"). Ask which one, and you'll know how to intervene.
