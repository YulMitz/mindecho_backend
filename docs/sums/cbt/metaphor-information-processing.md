## Using a Metaphor to Explain Information Processing

The screen metaphor is a powerful, concrete way to help clients understand how their negative core beliefs filter information in their environment. It explains why they seem unable to "see" evidence that contradicts what they believe about themselves.

### How the Screen Works

When a negative core belief becomes activated (typically during depression), imagine there's a screen around the client's head with that belief written on it a billion times. Any information that fits with the belief passes right through the screen into their mind. They register it, remember it, and use it as evidence.

But information that contradicts the belief doesn't fit through the screen. The client either:
- Doesn't notice it at all
- Sees it but immediately discounts it
- Reinterprets it to fit the negative belief

### The Client's Experience

From the client's perspective, they're being rational and realistic. If they don't see positive evidence, it must not exist. But what's actually happening is that the screen is blocking evidence from reaching their awareness or from being accepted as meaningful.

### Validating the Experience

It's important to help clients understand this isn't a character flaw or stupidity—it's how activated belief systems work. The screen is automatic. When depression activates the belief "I'm incompetent," the filtering happens without conscious effort.

### Testing the Metaphor

Help the client recognize when the screen is operating by reviewing recent events:
- What competent things did they do this week?
- How did they respond to those experiences? Did they dismiss them, minimize them, or reinterpret them?
- Can they see the screen filtering out the positive information?

Examples: "You helped your neighbor but thought 'anyone could have done that.' That's the screen. You brushed your teeth and got meals and showed up—but you wouldn't give yourself credit for that, would you? That's the screen operating too."

Once clients recognize the pattern, they can begin to question the screen and consciously look for evidence it filters out.
