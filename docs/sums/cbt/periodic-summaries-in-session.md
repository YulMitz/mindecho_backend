## Periodic Summaries Throughout Sessions

**Purpose**: Summaries serve three distinct functions. They ensure you've understood correctly, consolidate learning so clients can remember and practice it, and keep the session organized and on track.

### Type 1: Content Summaries (During Discussion)

When a client has described an issue with many details, summarize it back in the form of the cognitive model. This accomplishes several things:
- Confirms your understanding
- Presents their experience in a clearer, more organized way
- Keeps the most important cognitions and emotions activated in their mind
- Prepares them for the next step (evaluation, problem-solving, etc.)

**Example**:
"Let me make sure I understand, Abe. When you thought about sorting the mail this week, you thought 'I can't face doing this,' and you felt really down, so you've been avoiding this job. Is that right? Did you feel any other emotion or have any other automatic thoughts?"

**Key tip**: Use the client's own words as much as possible. If you paraphrase, you risk:
- Changing the intensity of the thought or emotion
- Making the client feel misunderstood
- Reducing the effectiveness of later intervention
