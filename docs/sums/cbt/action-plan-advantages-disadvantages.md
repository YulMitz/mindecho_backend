## Examining Advantages and Disadvantages

When clients are ambivalent about an Action Plan, use a structured cost-benefit analysis.

**The process:** Collaboratively list:
- Advantages of doing the Action Plan
- Disadvantages of doing it
- Advantages of not doing it
- Disadvantages of not doing it

Then ask them to weigh which factors matter most.

**Important nuance—the short-term relief trap:** Many depressed clients predict they'll feel better by *not* doing something (e.g., staying in bed). Validate this: "First you feel better, right? But how do you feel when you get up at noon?" Help them see the bigger picture and longer-term consequences.

**Linking to goals:** Ask: "Does staying in bed get you closer to your long-term goals or further away?" A client recognized: "I always think I'll feel better staying in bed, but I only feel good for a few minutes. Then I feel worse because I haven't done anything."

**For intractable doubt:** Acknowledge uncertainty honestly: "I don't know for sure that doing this will help." Then ask:
- "What will you lose if it doesn't work?"
- "What could be the potential gain in the long run if it does work?"
- "Haven't you already done the experiment of not getting up before lunch? How usually turns out?"

This shifts the framing from "You should do this" to "Let's test what actually works for you."
