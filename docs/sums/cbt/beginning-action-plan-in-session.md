## Beginning Action Plans In Session

When applicable, have clients start Action Plan items *right in the session*. This serves multiple purposes:

**Assessing capability:** If you'd like clients to complete worksheets, have them do one in session first. If they can't fill out a thought record with you there to help, they won't manage it at home. This reveals real obstacles—confusion about instructions, difficulty with the cognitive model, time management, or interfering thoughts—so you can address them immediately.

**Increasing follow-through:** Continuing a task is far easier than initiating it. The hardest moment is the period just before starting. Clients often describe this as the biggest barrier. By breaking through that inertia together in session, you make home completion much more likely. Momentum carries over.

**Establishing clarity:** Starting in session confirms the client understands what to do, when, and how long it takes. It eliminates ambiguity that might cause them to skip the task at home.

**Practical example:** If you want them to do a daily mood log, have them complete the first entry together in session. Show them how long it actually takes (often less than they fear). Then they can continue at home with confidence and a concrete example to reference.
