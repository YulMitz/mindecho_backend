## The Peaceful Schools Project: A Mentalizing Intervention

The Peaceful Schools Project is a systemic, school-wide intervention designed to transform a coercive, violent social system into a non-violent, mentalizing one. It operates as a philosophy rather than a rigid program, aiming to create a social environment that is fundamentally incompatible with interpersonal aggression. The core idea is that individuals are incapable of exercising aggression when they are actively mentalizing their victim's distress.

The approach combines a mentalizing perspective with an understanding of social power dynamics (the bully-victim-bystander triangle). The goal is to make the entire school community—students, teachers, staff, and administrators—more psychologically aware, so that the rules and daily practices of the system embody mentalizing principles.

### Key Components of the Intervention

1.  **Positive Climate Campaigns:** This involves a conscious effort to shift the language and thinking of everyone in the school. Posters, magnets, and counselor-led discussions introduce concepts like the bully-victim-bystander dynamic and healthy ways to handle anger. This provides a shared vocabulary for identifying and resolving problems without resorting to coercion. The focus is on the *relationship* between the roles, not just on blaming the bully.

2.  **Classroom Management (Discipline Plan):** A single child's disruptive behavior is conceptualized as a problem for the *whole class*. When a disruption occurs, the teacher stops and facilitates a class discussion, assigning the roles of bully, victim, and bystander to understand the dynamic. This reduces scapegoating and promotes insight. For repeat issues, the entire class might fill out a "power-struggle referral alert," defining the behavior collectively. This approach encourages the disruptive child to develop a mental representation of their experience without feeling punished, which would inhibit mentalizing.
