## MBT Conceptualization of Eating Disorders: Mentalizing Problems and Treatment Rationale

### Mentalizing Profile in Eating Disorders

Research shows considerable evidence of mentalizing problems in patients with eating disorders. The characteristic pattern involves:

**Distorted modes of thinking (especially about body, weight, shape):**
- **Psychic equivalence thinking** — the belief that thoughts and internal experiences are literally true and unchangeable (e.g., "I feel fat, therefore I am fat")
- **Teleological mode** — action-oriented thinking focused only on concrete outcomes without understanding underlying mental states or intentions
- **Pretend mode** — thinking that is disconnected from reality and emotional meaning

These modes dominate particularly in relation to the individual's body weight and shape, creating a rigid, non-reflective relationship to physical self.

### Developmental Understanding

MBT-ED assumes:
- **Developmental vulnerabilities** emerge especially during adolescence
- **Environmental factors** interact with biological predispositions
- **Social anxieties and relational difficulties** often precede or accompany eating pathology
- **Eating disorder symptoms serve a function** — they represent attempts at social self-regulation when mentalizing capacity breaks down in interpersonal contexts

### The Core Problem: Social Retreat and Lost Mentalizing

When adolescents or young adults with emerging eating disorders withdraw from social interaction (due to social anxiety, relational trauma, or peer difficulties), they simultaneously lose access to the regulatory function that mentalizing provides. Eating behaviors and body-focused preoccupation become substitutes for authentic emotional and interpersonal connection.

### MBT-ED Treatment Structure

A **combined program of individual and group psychotherapy** addresses this dual challenge:

**Individual sessions:**
- Build the therapeutic relationship and mentalizing capacity in a contained dyad
- Work on social and relational adaptation
- Support resilience

**Group sessions:**
- Directly expose and address social anxieties
- Practice mentalizing within a peer context
- Create experience of being understood and accepted by others

### Core Foci of MBT-ED

1. **Social and relational adaptation** — helping patients re-engage with authentic peer relationships and move away from isolation
2. **Improving low mentalizing triggered by social retreat** — restoring the capacity to think about self and others when social anxiety has caused shutdown
3. **Supporting resilience** — building capacity to manage interpersonal stress without retreating into eating symptoms

### Physical Health Integration

MBT-ED pays appropriate attention to physical problems resulting from the eating disorder (malnutrition, organ damage, medical risk), but the psychological work focuses on mentalizing and relational function rather than food/weight management alone.
