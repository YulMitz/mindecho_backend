## Responding to Mentalizing Breakdown in MBT-C

When a child's capacity to think about mental states collapses — usually triggered by heightened affect, misunderstanding, or overwhelm — the therapist has a structured sequence of responses adapted from adult MBT, calibrated for the child's developmental stage.

### Step 1: Empathy and Support (During Dysregulation)

**Timing:** Use immediately when affect is high and dysregulation is active

**What to do:**
- Reduce the child's arousal and distress
- Offer support in a non-judgmental way
- Validate the child's experience
- Do NOT attempt explicit mentalizing or exploration at this moment

**How it looks:**
- Warm, reassuring tone
- Physical safety (if safe to do so, proximity; if not, clear boundary-setting)
- Simple, concrete language
- Acknowledgment: "I can see this is really hard"

The goal is **re-regulation**, not understanding.

### Step 2: Stop and Rewind (After Regulation Restored)

**Timing:** Only after mentalizing has been partly restored through empathy and support
