## Using the Half-Smile to Tolerate Distress

The "half-smile" is a DBT Distress-Tolerance skill designed to help clients bear unavoidable pain without resorting to behaviors that will make the situation worse. It is a powerful, physically-based intervention that can restore the capacity to mentalize when it has been eroded by intense negative emotion.

**The Skill:**
The half-smile is a Duchenne smile. It involves a gentle upturning of the corners of the mouth, but crucially, the smile must also reach the eyes. This is achieved through a slight elevation of the cheeks and a gentle crinkling of the skin at the outer corners of the eyes.

**The Rationale (from Positive Psychology):**
We can explain to clients that this is more than just "putting on a happy face." It's a way to use the body to change the mind.
1.  **Undoing Stress:** Research by Barbara Fredrickson shows that positive experiences, even induced ones like smiling, can quell and undo the cardiovascular stress caused by negative emotions. A genuine smile can lead to a more rapid slowing of heart rate and a return to baseline blood pressure.
2.  **Activating Pleasure:** EEG research by Paul Ekman and Richard Davidson demonstrates that a Duchenne smile (contracting both mouth and eye muscles) activates brain patterns in the left frontal region associated with pleasure. By deliberately arranging their face in a half-smile, clients can directly influence their brain activity and emotional state.
