## Introducing the Concept of Mentalizing

Mentalizing is a core human capacity that we use every day, often without realizing it. We can introduce it to clients with simple phrases like "keeping mind in mind," or "attending to mental states." It's the process of making sense of our own behavior and the behavior of others by imagining the thoughts, feelings, wishes, and beliefs that might be behind it.

A simple example can make this clear: Imagine seeing a stranger on a street corner, map in hand, with a furrowed brow, looking down one street then another. You would likely infer they are lost and confused. That inference—imagining their internal state based on their behavior—is mentalizing in action.

### The Scope of Mentalizing

Mentalizing involves awareness of both thoughts and feelings, in both ourselves and in others. It's more than just empathy, which primarily focuses on others' feelings. Mentalizing includes understanding our own thoughts and feelings, too. It's not just about thinking clearly; it’s also about *feeling clearly*. The goal is to develop an integrated awareness of our full internal world.
