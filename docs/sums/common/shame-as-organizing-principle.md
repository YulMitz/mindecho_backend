## Shame: Recognizing and Counteracting Its Disorganizing Effect

Shame is often invisible in therapy—not because it is rare, but because it silences people. When shame is activated, a person loses temporary access to their coping skills, problem-solving abilities, and authentic self-expression. They become preoccupied with how they are being perceived and with avoiding further humiliation.
