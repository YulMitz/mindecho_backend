## Skillful Means: Balancing Doing Mind and Being Mind

One of the central paradoxes of effective living is learning to balance action and acceptance, striving and surrendering. **Doing mind** and **being mind** represent this fundamental polarity.

### Definitions

**Doing mind** ("something-to-do mind"):
- Focused on achieving goals, solving problems, planning, evaluating
- Driven by reason and logic; practical and pragmatic
- Essential for building, creating, solving problems, following through on commitments
- Problem: can become compulsive, exhausting, dismissive of present joy and relational intimacy

**Being mind** ("nothing-to-do mind"):
- Focused on present experiencing without agenda for change
- Contemplative, receptive, accepting of what is
- Allows rest, presence, appreciation, and connection
- Problem: in excess, can become passive, avoidant of necessary action, or stuck in rumination

### The Spiritual Framing: Martha and Mary

The Biblical story illustrates this tension: Martha is consumed with preparing and doing, distracted from being present with Jesus. Mary chooses "the better part"—sitting at his feet and listening. Both have value; both are necessary for a full life.

- **Contemplative life path** = being mind
- **Active life path** = doing mind
- **Wise mind** = synthesis of both

### Why Balance Matters

Without doing mind, people cannot build stability, solve problems, or move toward valued goals. Without being mind, people burn out, miss intimate moments, and remain unconscious of their lives. A balanced life requires both.

### Recognizing Imbalance in Sessions

**Excess doing mind:**
- Client describes constant activity, productivity obsession, difficulty relaxing
- Relationships suffer due to task-focus
- Anxiety about what's not done; difficulty being present with loved ones
- Workaholism or perfectionism patterns

**Excess being mind:**
- Client avoids necessary action or problem-solving
- Rationalized as "acceptance" but is actually avoidance or dissociation
- Stuck in rumination without moving toward change
- Difficulty following through on goals or commitments

### Skillful Means in Practice

**For doing-mind-dominant clients:** Teach being-mind skills—mindful breathing, present-moment awareness during meals or with loved ones, brief meditation, appreciation of small moments. Validate that rest is productive; presence is a skill.

**For being-mind-dominant clients:** Teach activation, behavioral scheduling, and intentional goal-pursuit. Help them distinguish wise acceptance (acknowledging what cannot change) from avoidant acceptance (giving up on things they can influence).

**The integration:** Wise mind brings the two together. A person can set goals (doing mind) *and* accept the process without clinging to outcomes (being mind). One can work hard (doing) *and* remain present to the moment (being). This is skillful means.
