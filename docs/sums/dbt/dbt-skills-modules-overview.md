## DBT Skills Modules: Framework and Purpose

DBT skills training is organized into four core modules, each addressing a different consequence of emotion dysregulation. Skills are taught sequentially but can be reviewed, revisited, and applied in any order depending on client need.

### Module 1: Mindfulness Skills

**Purpose**: Address dysregulation of the sense of self and distorted reality perception.

Clients with high emotion dysregulation often view reality through the lens of their emotions rather than as it actually is. They may also have no stable sense of self, feeling empty or disconnected. Mindfulness skills teach:

- Conscious observation of internal and external experience without judgment
- Distinguishing between emotion and reality
- Awareness of the present moment
- "Wise mind": balancing emotional and rational thinking

These skills are foundational and are reviewed at the start of each subsequent module.

### Module 2: Interpersonal Effectiveness Skills

**Purpose**: Address interpersonal dysregulation and chaotic relationships.

Clients with emotion dysregulation often have intense, unstable relationships marked by fear of abandonment, intense conflict, or withdrawal. These skills teach:

- **DEAR MAN**: Assertiveness and effective communication (Describe, Express, Assert, Reinforce; stay Mindful, Appear confident, Negotiate)
- **GIVE**: Relationship maintenance and respect (be Gentle, act Interested, Validate, use an Easy manner)
- **FAST**: Self-respect and value alignment (be Fair, no Apologies, Stick to values, be Truthful)

These address the fundamental truth: effective relationships require both a stable sense of self and the ability to communicate needs clearly while respecting others.

### Module 3: Emotion Regulation Skills

**Purpose**: Address emotional dysregulation directly—teaching recognition, management, and modification of emotions.

This module teaches clients to:

- Identify and name emotions (many dysregulated clients struggle to label feelings)
- Change emotional responses through behavioral action (opposite action, problem-solving)
- Check the facts to reality-test emotional interpretations
- Reduce vulnerability to emotional flooding through self-care (ABC PLEASE: Accumulate positive emotions, Build mastery, Cope ahead, physical health)
- Manage extreme emotions through mindfulness and reality acceptance

### Module 4: Distress Tolerance Skills

**Purpose**: Address behavioral dyscontrol by teaching crisis survival and acceptance of what cannot be changed.

This module is critical because it teaches the client to survive emotional pain without engaging in dysregulation-driven behaviors. Skills include:

- **STOP skill**: Pause automatic reactions during crisis
- **TIPP**: Physiological techniques to shift emotional state (Temperature, Intense exercise, Paced breathing, Paired muscle relaxation)
- **ACCEPTS**: Distraction using all five senses (Activities, Contributing, Comparisons, Emotions, Pushing away, Thoughts, Sensations)
- **IMPROVE the moment**: Coping strategies for temporary management
- **Radical acceptance**: Accepting painful reality when it cannot be changed
- **Turning the mind & willing hands**: Commitment strategies for acceptance

### Balance of Acceptance and Change

All four modules contain both **acceptance skills** (learning to tolerate and accept what is) and **change skills** (taking action to modify thoughts, emotions, or situations). For any problem, an effective approach may include acceptance as well as change. This dialectical balance is central to DBT.

### Teaching Structure

Each module is divided into manageable sections. Complex skills like DEAR MAN are broken into smaller component parts to increase comprehension and prevent overwhelm. Clients work on one skill or skill set at a time, incorporating mastered skills into work on new modules.

This modular structure allows flexibility: a therapist or skills trainer can adjust pacing, revisit difficult skills, or adjust the sequence based on what the client needs most urgently.
