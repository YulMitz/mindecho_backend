## Satiation: Preventing Motivation by Meeting the Need First

Satiation is the strategy of providing a reinforcer *before* the problematic behavior occurs, which reduces the motivation for that behavior. Instead of waiting for the unwanted behavior to appear and then extinguishing it, you satisfy the person's need or desire preemptively.

### How Satiation Works

When a reinforcer is given *non-contingently* (not tied to the unwanted behavior), the person's motivation for seeking it through problematic means decreases. The need is already met, so there's no urgency.

**Simple example:**
- A hungry baby will cry when hungry. If you feed the baby *before* the hunger cry starts, the baby doesn't cry.
- A spouse who gets angry when you forget to buy toothpaste won't get angry if toothpaste is already in the house.
- A lonely sister who complains constantly about loneliness may complain less if you call her regularly *without* waiting for her to complain first.

### Satiation vs. Extinction: Key Differences

| Aspect | Extinction | Satiation |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| When reinforcer is given | Withheld when behavior occurs | Given before behavior occurs |
| Behavioral burst | Yes—behavior escalates initially | No—motivation is reduced, no burst |
| Client burden | High—must tolerate escalation | Low—need is already met |
| Timeline | Slower; behavior decreases over time | Faster; motivation never builds |
