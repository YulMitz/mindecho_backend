## Six Levels of Validation: Practical Hierarchy

These six levels form a hierarchy—each builds on the previous one, and each subsequent level is stronger. They're drawn from DBT individual therapy training and adapted for skills teaching. Not every situation requires all six; use them flexibly based on what the client needs.

### Level 1: Pay Attention

Simply treat the person as relevant and meaningful. Make them visible and seen. Be mindful of your own nonverbal reactions—avoid eye-rolling, looking away, saying "I don't care what you say."

Paying attention communicates: *You count. You matter right now.*

This is not agreement or approval. It's acknowledging the person is alive. When we ignore someone, we say: your activities, feelings, beliefs are not important
