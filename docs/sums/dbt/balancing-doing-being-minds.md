## Balancing Doing Mind and Being Mind

In DBT mindfulness work, **doing mind** and **being mind** represent two complementary but opposite mental states. Both are necessary for a balanced, wise life. When either dominates, it distorts reality and creates suffering.

### Doing Mind: Goal-Achievement Focus

**Doing mind** is task-focused, goal-oriented, and comparative. It views thoughts as facts and constantly measures current reality against desired future states. In healthy doses, doing mind enables productivity, planning, and goal pursuit.

**When doing mind is excessive**, it becomes "driven mind"—relentless ambition and activity. People get caught in:
- **Workaholism**: endless productivity without enjoyment; intense drive to always be doing something
- **Perfectionism**: constant evaluation and comparison; never satisfied
- **Addiction**: rigid focus on a compulsive activity while awareness of consequences and loved ones narrows
- **Automatic pilot**: habitual action without noticing context changes; easy to make mistakes

Clients stuck in doing mind often report missing their own lives, never stopping to "smell the roses," losing awareness of relationships, and experiencing guilt or shame about rest.

### Being Mind: Present-Moment Experience

**Being mind** is open, curious, and non-evaluative. Thoughts are treated as sensations arising and passing. The focus is on immediate, direct experience without judgment of past or future—"just this moment."

**When being mind is deficient**, people miss their lives entirely—they focus so much on future comparisons and past regrets (what doing mind encourages) that they never experience present joy. This often manifests as feeling life has passed them by.

**When being mind is excessive**, it becomes indulgent and self-centered. The person may neglect responsibilities, refuse to plan, or avoid tasks that need doing.

### The Synthesis: Skillful Means

**Wise mind balances both.** The goal is **doing what is needed while experiencing fully the uniqueness of each moment**. This is called "skillful means"—effective action paired with presence. A client might work toward a goal (doing mind) while remaining fully aware of sensations, people, and the present moment (being mind).

### Teaching & Assessment

Ask clients: 
- Do you find yourself always needing to be productive? Guilty at rest?
- Or do you struggle to follow through on goals, preferring to "live in the now"?
- When was the last time you *enjoyed* an accomplishment, or noticed your own life happening?

Practice exercises on Handout 9a include bringing being-mind moments into daily activities and consciously shifting between modes as needed.
