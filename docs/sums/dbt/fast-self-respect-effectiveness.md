## FAST: Skills for Maintaining Self-Respect

FAST skills address how to pursue your goals in a way that lets you respect yourself afterward—even if you don't get what you want.

### (Be) Fair

Be fair to both yourself and the other person. Validate your own feelings and wishes, not just theirs. It's hard to respect yourself if you're always giving in or never standing up for what matters to you.

**Why this matters**: Self-respect erodes when you consistently sacrifice your needs or take advantage of others. A balanced approach—honoring both your needs and theirs—builds long-term self-respect.

**The trap**: Some clients prioritize everyone else's needs indefinitely and wonder why they feel invisible or resentful. Others justify taking advantage by telling themselves they "deserve" it. Neither builds genuine self-respect.

### (No) Apologies

Don't overapologize. When apologies are warranted, offer them sincerely. But don't apologize for:
- Having an opinion
- Making a request
- Disagreeing
- Simply existing

Excessive apologies undermine your sense of mastery and competence. They imply you're always in the wrong—that you shouldn't have spoken up in the first place.

**The nuance**: One sincere apology for a genuine mistake is appropriate and can strengthen relationships. Chronic apologies ("Sorry, but I was wondering if maybe...") erode both self-respect and relationship effectiveness.

### Stick to Values

Know what your moral or valued way of acting is, and hold onto that position. Don't sell out your integrity to get approval or reach an objective.

**The complexity**: Some clients have black-and-white thinking: either they'll sacrifice everything for approval, or they treat every situation as if their values are on trial. The goal is clarity about what truly matters to you, combined with flexibility about the context.

**Values in relationships**: Conflicts between what others want you to do and your moral code are common. It's very difficult to maintain self-respect when you repeatedly violate your own values in a relationship. Over time, this erodes not just your self-respect but also the relationship itself.

**When values compete**: In dire situations or when lives are at stake, people might choose to bend their values. The skill is making that choice *mindfully*, not out of panic or desperation.

### (Be) Truthful

Don't lie, exaggerate, or act helpless when you're capable. A pattern of dishonesty erodes self-respect over time.
