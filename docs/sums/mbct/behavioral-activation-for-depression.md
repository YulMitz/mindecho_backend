## Behavioral Activation and the Antidepressant Activity List

When depression begins to creep in, motivation evaporates. The depressed mind says "I don't feel like doing anything," which then becomes the excuse for doing nothing—which deepens the depression. Behavioral activation interrupts this cycle.

### Two Categories of Helpful Activity

The MBCT approach organizes helpful activities into two categories:

**Pleasure-based activities** directly counteract anhedonia. They're selected because the person *enjoys* them or because they provide comfort:
- Phone a friend
- Rent a video you enjoy
- Have a hot bath
- Treat yourself to favorite food
- Go for a walk (with a friend or dog)
- Listen to music that makes you feel good
- Spend time with someone you like

**Mastery/achievement activities** provide a sense of control and accomplishment:
- Clean the house or clear out a space
- Catch up with letter writing or email
- Do work or schoolwork
- Pay a bill
- Do gardening
- Tackle something you've been procrastinating on
- Take exercise
- Cook a meal

### The Critical Instruction

The instruction accompanying these activities is essential: **"Although you may not feel like doing any of these, select at least one and do it anyway."**

This directly addresses the motivational barrier. The depressed mind produces the thought "I don't feel like it," and then uses that feeling as justification for inaction. The instruction says: the feeling is not the signal to wait. The presence of low mood is the signal to *act*.

### Breaking Down Large Tasks

For clients whose task initiation is severely impaired, there's a meta-instruction: **"Remember to take that big task and break it down into smaller steps (e.g., just do 10 minutes of it) and congratulate yourself afterwards."**

A task that feels impossible—"clean the whole house"—becomes manageable—"spend 10 minutes clearing one shelf." The accomplishment, however small, provides feedback that you can do things even when depressed.

### When to Consult the List

The Antidepressant Activity List is something participants write or receive during the program, *while they're still well*, and then **file away until they need to consult it**.
