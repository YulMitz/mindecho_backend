## Opening with Practice: The Foundation of Each Session

In MBCT, every class from Session 2 onward begins with formal meditation practice—not discussion, not homework review. This is a deliberate and essential departure from typical cognitive therapy, where you might set an agenda, check on assignments, and collaboratively solve problems.

**Why practice comes first:**

The shift from "doing" mode to "being" mode is central to MBCT's approach. When participants arrive for Session 2, they're naturally eager to compare notes and share their week. The room is filled with conversation. While it would be natural (and faster) to launch into discussion of how the homework went, this would reinforce the very mental habits the program aims to change: rumination, analysis, and problem-solving at the expense of direct experience.

By starting with a body scan or sitting meditation, the instructor invokes a different mind state. This practice-first foundation:

- **Anchors awareness in the present moment** rather than rehashing the past week or anticipating solutions
- **Models the shift from "doing" to "being"** — the very distinction participants are learning to recognize in themselves
- **Creates a container** where whatever arises during discussion can be explored with the same curious, observant stance cultivated in meditation
- **Grounds the class** after participants transition from their busy weeks

**Managing the tension:**

Even experienced instructors can feel pressure to "get on with it" — to address participants' concerns, reduce their distress, and solve problems. But jumping straight to discussion can inadvertently reinforce the idea that mindfulness is just another tool for achieving goals or "fixing" things. 

The practice-first approach allows themes that emerge during meditation (wandering mind, judgment, difficulty, boredom, discomfort) to be explored naturally in the discussion that follows. These experiences become the teaching material, not obstacles to get past.
